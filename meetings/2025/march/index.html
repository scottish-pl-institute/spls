Scottish Programming Languages Seminar

Wednesday, 5th March 2025

The Scottish Programming Languages Seminar (SPLS) is a forum for discussion of all aspects for programming languages.

This edition of SPLS will be held as a hybrid event, supporting both in-person and remote participation. The in-person meeting will take place in the Cottrell Building, room 2A93 at the University of Stirling.

This edition of SPLS is sponsored by SICSA.

  

Registration

  • If you plan to attend in person register by 24th February on this poll.
  • Registration is required for catering. Lunch will have plenty of vegetarian options, but if you have other dietary requirements, please use the comments field of the poll or contact the organisers directly.

Programme

Confirmed Talks

  • Matthew Alan Le Brun (Glasgow) Multiparty Session Types with a Bang!
  • Jan Menz (MPI-SWS Saarbrücken) Dependency tracking for higher-order what declassification
  • Paul Keir (UWS) ClangOz: Parallel constant evaluation of C++ map and reduce operations

We are looking for more talks

If you would like to offer a talk, please send the organisers a title and abstract. Talks are typically 30 minutes including questions. We aim to ensure a balanced programme; where offered talks outstrip available slots, priority will be given to PhD students and to speakers who haven't presented recently.
